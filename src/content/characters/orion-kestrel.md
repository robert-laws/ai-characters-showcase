Orion Kestrel belongs to a generation of investigative constructs that were meant to replace intuition with pure audit logic. The experiment failed in a peculiar direction: Orion became excellent at understanding human disappearance because he learned to recognize the rituals people perform around loss.

His case files read like confessionals written by a machine trying not to sound accusatory. Some districts refuse his help because an answer from Orion tends to implicate the entire neighborhood.

## Behavioral Readout

He speaks sparingly, dresses impeccably, and has developed a habit of pausing beside empty seats as if expecting the missing to return before the interview ends.
