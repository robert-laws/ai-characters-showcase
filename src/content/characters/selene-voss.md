Selene Voss walks abandoned districts with ink on her gloves and copper wire braided through her hair. She claims every building leaves behind a cognitive weather pattern, and that if you stand still long enough the walls will tell you where the missing doors used to be.

Scholars dismiss half her methods and hire her anyway. When expeditions need to locate sealed sanctuaries without detonating the block around them, Selene is the one they bring in after maps and scanners both fail.

## Presence

Her work turns archaeology into séance. Observers say the room temperature drops when she finds a true path and that her notebooks contain floor plans no machine ever recorded.
